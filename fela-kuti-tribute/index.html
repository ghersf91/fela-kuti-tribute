Fela Kuti

The father of Afrobeat

Fela Kuti smiles at the audience of a concert in Utrecht, Netherlands in 1988.

Here's a timeline of Fela Kuti's life:

  • 1938: Born in Abeokuta, Western Region, British Nigeria (now Abeokuta, Ogun State, Nigeria)
  • 1958: Sent to London by his family to study Medicine but decided to study music instead at the Trinity College of Music, with the trumpet being his preferred instrument
  • 1960: Married his first wife Remilekun (Remi) Taylor, with whom he had three children (Femi, Yeni and Sola)
  • 1963: Moved back to newly independent Nigeria, re-formed his old band (Koola Lobitos), and trained as a radio producer for the Nigerian Broadcasting Corporation
  • 1967: Moved to Ghana to look for a new musical direction. Named his new style Afrobeat, a combination of highlife, funk, jazz, salsa, calypso and traditional Yoruba music
  • 1969: Took the band to Los Angeles for ten months, where he discovered the Black Power movement. Renamed the band Nigeria '70, which was later changed again to Afrika '70
  • 1970: Returned from the United States and formed the "Kalakuta Republic" in Lagos, a communal compound that housed his family, band members and recording studio. The Kalakuta Reppublic was declared independent from Nigeria
  • 1977: After the release of the album Zombie, which heavily criticized the military government, the Kalakuta Republic was burnt to the ground by more than a thousand armed soldiers
  • 1978: Married 27 women (dancers, composers and singers with whom he worked with), not only to mark the anniversary of the burning of the Kalakuta Republic, but to protect Kuti and his wives from authorities' false claims that he was kidnapping women
  • 1979: Put himself forward as a candidate for president in Nigeria's first elections in decades, but his candidacy was refused. Changed the name of his band to Egypt '80
  • 1984: Jailed on a charge of currency smuggling. Amnesty International and other organizations denounced the case as politically motivated. He was released after 20 months
  • 1993: Arrested and charged, along four members of Afrika '70, for the murder of an electrician
  • 1997: Dies in Lagos from complications related to AIDS, although his widow denied this cause of death
  • 2021: His band, lead by his younger son Seun, is still active, releasing music under the moniker Seun Kuti & Egypt '80
